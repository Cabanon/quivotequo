Land use, land use change and forestry (LULUCF)

A9-0161/2022 - Ville Niinistö - Amendements de la commission compétente - votes séparés - Am 17/3

Détail de l'amendement

(5) In order to contribute ensure to that the increased LULUCF ambition sector makes a sustainable and predictable long-term contribution to reduce the greenhouse Union gas climate net neutrality emissions objective from by 2050 at least the 40 latest % and to at the least aim 55 of % achieving below negative 1990 emissions levels thereafter , binding annual targets for net greenhouse gas removals should be set out for each Member State in the land LULUCF use, sector land in use the change period and up forestry to sector at in least 2050, starting with the period from 2026 to 2030 (in analogy to the annual emission allocations set out in Regulation (EU) 2018/842 of the European Parliament and of the Council32 ), resulting in a an intermediary target of at least 310 millions of tonnes CO2 equivalent of net removals for the Union as a whole in 2030 , as well as an additional target of at least 50 million tonnes CO2 equivalent of net removals to be achieved by additional measures and initiatives at Union and Member State level to support carbon farming measures based on rules at Union level, but on a voluntary basis at farm level, while ensuring that those additional net removals are not offsetting necessary emissions reductions in accordance with Union climate targets as laid down in Regulation (EU) 2021/1119 . The methodology used to establish the national targets for 2030 should take into account the average greenhouse gas emissions and removals from the years 2016, 2017 and 2018, reported by each Member State, and reflect the current mitigation performance of the land LULUCF use, land use change and forestry sector, and each Member State’s share of the managed land area in the Union, taking into account the capacity of that Member State to improve its performance in the sector via restoration, land management practices or changes in land use that benefit the climate and biodiversity.
